About the Agents

The Research Agent

The Research Agent is your team's expert investigator. Think of it as a specialist designed to handle complex research questions. When you ask it something that requires deep digging, it doesn't just perform a simple search.

Instead, it follows a more intelligent process:

  • It understands the query: It breaks down your complex question into smaller, more effective search terms.
  • It searches you vector store: It looks for information in the vector store you created.
  • It finds the best information: After gathering data, it intelligently sorts and ranks the results to find the most relevant pieces of information.
  • It gives you a clear answer: Finally, it synthesizes all the key information into a single, comprehensive, and easy-to-understand response.

The Flow Super Agent

The Flow Super Agent acts as a project manager or a conductor for a team of other specialized agents. Its job is not to do the research itself, but to orchestrate a workflow between other agents.

You can design a specific, step-by-step plan for it to follow. For example, you could create a workflow where the Flow Super Agent instructs other agents to:

Super Agent

Financial Analyst

Orchestrates a financial analysis by comparing an internal Accenture fiscal report with external market and competitor data.

Utility Agents

Fiscal Report Researcher

Analyzes Accenture's provided fiscal report to extract key financial data and performance metrics.

Market Analyst

Researches current market trends in the IT services and consulting industries, focusing on factors relevant to Accenture.

Competitor Analyst

Researches the financial performance and recent activities of Accenture's key competitors, such as Deloitte, PwC, Capgemini, and IBM Consulting.

Comparative Analysis Writer

Writes a comparative analysis report by synthesizing findings from Accenture's fiscal report, market analysis, and competitor research.

This allows us to build powerful, automated, multi-step processes to handle complex tasks in a structured and repeatable way, much like an assembly line.
